About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Statistics at Ohio State. My advisor is Dr. Yoonkyung Lee and I am interested in machine learning applications in statistics. We are working on dimensionality reduction, matrix factorization, and classification with large binary datasets. Last summer, I was a fellow in the Eric & Wendy Schmidt Data Science for Social Good Fellowship at the University of Chicago.

I work as a graduate research associate for the Campus Transit Lab in the Department of Civil, Environmental and Geodetic Engineering with Dr. Prem Goel, Dr. Rabi Mishalani, and Dr. Mark McCord. There, we are developing methods to estimate origin-destination patterns of travelers on public transportation. I am also a statistical consultant for IGS Energy, where I am in charge of maintaining, evaluating, and updating electricity load forecasting methodologies.

I was part of the team that won the 2013 Capital One Student Modeling Competition, using matrix factorization in our solution. Find out more about me from a small profile I wrote for the OSU Statistics student blog, a story that the College of Arts and Sciences did on my summer fellowship, and an interview I had with Amstat News.
