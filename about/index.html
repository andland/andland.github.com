About Me

Jan 16th, 2014

I am a graduate student in the Department of Statistics at Ohio State. I work with Dr. Yoonkung Lee and am interested in machine learning applications in statistics. We are working on dimensionality reduction, matrix factorization, and classification with large binary datasets. I was part of the team that won the 2013 Capital One Student Modeling Competition, using matrix factorization in our solution. You can read more about it here.

I work as a graduate research associate for the Campus Transit Lab in the Department of Civil, Environmental and Geodetic Engineering with Dr. Prem Goel, Dr. Rabi Mishalani, and Dr. Mark McCord. There, I have mainly been working on two projects. One is developing methods to estimate origin-destination patterns of travelers on public transportation. The other is assessing the impact the public transportation use has on greenhouse gas emissions.

I am also a statistical consultant for IGS Energy, where I am in charge of maintaining, evaluating, and updating electricity load forecasting methodologies.

I wrote a small profile for the OSU Statistics student blog here.
